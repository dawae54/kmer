Gets the distance between this \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile} object ( $P_1$) and the given argument object {\ttfamily other\+Profile} ( $P_2$). The distance between two Profiles $P_1$ and $P_2$ is calculated in the following way\+: 

$d = \frac{ \sum_{kmer_i(P_1)} | rank_{kmer_i(P_1)}^{P_1} -
rank_{kmer_i(P_1)}^{P_2} | }{size(P_1) * size(P_2) }$,

where $kmer_i(p_j)$ is the kmer $i$ of the \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile}  $p_j,
j \in \{1, 2\}$ and $rank_{kmer_i(p_j)}^{p_k}$ is the ranking of the kmer $i$ of the \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile} $p_j, j \in \{1, 2\}$ in the \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile} $p_k$.

The rank of a kmer is the position in which it appears in the list of \doxylink{classKmerFreq}{Kmer\+Freq}. We consider 0 as the first position (rank equals to 0). When calculating $rank_{kmer_i(P_1)}^{P_2}$, if the kmer $kmer_i(P_1)$ does not appears in the \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile} $P_2$ we consider that the rank is equals to the size of \doxylink{classProfile}{Profile} $P_2$.
